Understand the Essence

You need to understand what customers are saying, and the easiest way is to make a word cloud. Take all the pluses from the reviews from websites, combine them, make a "cloud", weed out unnecessary words - and there you have a picture that gives you an understanding of what customers are talking about.

What good do we do?

The goal is to fail to see what problems customers "hire" your product to solve. It's easy to make a functional JTBD out of reviews: there's action, object, and context. Context is not very interesting for us, we need to define objects and action verbs. To understand what users do with objects we can use textual analysis - concordance. This will help you "center" the individual words in the text array and see the context around them. Now I have objects and actions with them - you can read.

Explain the Benefit of Your Product

Read the comments further to make some connection between what clients do and what they get out of it. You'll get some pretty interesting conclusions. When they were improving the process, they were simplifying and speeding it up and thereby saving time.
